How Search Engines Understand a Page (Simple Explanation)

Updated: Jan 6, 2026 • Reading time: ~6 minutes

This page is a test input for the Web Simplifier AI Chrome extension. It contains real-looking article structure (headings, lists, code, callouts), plus extra noise like navigation and ads. A good extractor should keep the article and drop the noise.

1) What a crawler does

A web crawler is software that requests pages and downloads the HTML. It doesn’t “see” the page like a human at first; it reads markup and then may execute JavaScript. After fetching content, the crawler stores a copy and passes it to systems that analyze text, links, and structure.

2) Why clean main-content extraction matters

Most real webpages include lots of non-article text: menus, cookie banners, related posts, comment sections, and ads. If you send all of that to an AI model, you waste tokens and risk low-quality summaries. That’s why this extension first tries Readability and then falls back to a simpler main-content heuristic.

Key signals of a “main content” section

  • Long continuous text with paragraphs
  • Headings that match the page topic
  • Fewer repeated UI elements (nav links, buttons)
  • Located inside <article> or <main> tags
